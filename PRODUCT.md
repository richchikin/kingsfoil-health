# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Business leaders, CFOs, and HR professionals at companies frustrated by rising health insurance costs. They arrive desperate for alternatives — they know what they're paying is unsustainable but don't know what options exist. They're skeptical of the traditional broker model and looking for someone who actually understands their problem and has real solutions.

## Product Purpose

Kingsfoil Health's website educates employers on alternative health benefits strategies (level-funded plans, direct primary care, ICHRA, etc.) and positions the brokerage as a knowledgeable, caring guide — not a salesperson. Success means a visitor goes from "I'm stuck paying too much" to "these people understand my problem and have a plan" and books a consultation.

## Brand Personality

Professional, warm, educational.

- **Professional:** We know the regulatory landscape, the math, the carrier options. Expert confidence without jargon.
- **Warm:** We genuinely care about the businesses and employees affected. Approachable, not clinical.
- **Educational:** We lead with knowledge, not pressure. The site should feel like getting advice from a trusted advisor, not a sales pitch.

## Anti-references

- Generic insurance/broker sites with stock photos of handshakes and blue gradients
- Overly corporate, stiff, or institutional tone
- Slick/salesy feel with aggressive CTAs and hollow promises
- "Trusted partner" clichés and meaningless superlatives
- Walls of text on non-educational pages (educational content gets a pass for depth)
- Cookie-cutter SaaS landing page aesthetics

## Design Principles

1. **Options over overwhelm** — Show visitors there are real alternatives. Structure information so possibilities feel accessible, not daunting.
2. **Earned trust** — Demonstrate expertise through substance (data, frameworks, real explanations), not claims. Show, don't tell.
3. **Human first** — Every page should feel like a conversation with someone who cares, not a corporate brochure. Warmth in tone, warmth in design.
4. **Clarity is kindness** — Health insurance is confusing enough. Design and copy should reduce cognitive load, not add to it.
5. **Depth when it matters** — Educational content should be thorough and generous. Marketing pages should be concise and action-oriented.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Ensure sufficient color contrast, readable type sizes, keyboard navigation, and reduced-motion support. No known specific audience accommodations beyond standard best practices.
